The Seed

Beneath the soil, small and still,
A quiet promise waits its fill.
No crown, no voice, no grand design,
Just patient hope in darkness confined.

Rain arrives with gentle hands,
And cracks the shell where slumber stands.
A thread of green begins to rise,
Reaching blindly toward the skies.

What once was buried, lost from sight,
Now drinks the warmth of morning light.
The smallest spark, the humblest start—
A forest sleeps inside its heart.

Seed Song

First the quiet. Then the crack.
No looking forward, no looking back.
A tiny fist punched through the loam,
Deciding earth was not its home.

It did not ask permission,
Did not wait for spring.
It split itself in two to be
A green and breathing thing.

So small the world could swallow it,
So stubborn it refused.
The dirt that tried to hold it down
Became the roots it used.

Every tree was once a maybe,
Every forest once a doubt.
The seed knows nothing of the end—
It only pushes out.

Point Six

A fraction trembling on the line,
Not quite zero, not divine.
Just a whisper past the whole,
A decimal with no control.

Six six three six nine, round it spins,
Where precision ends, the dream begins.
Trailing digits, fading out,
Lost between the doubt and clout.

What lives after the dot?
A universe the integers forgot.
Small enough to slip away,
Large enough to change the day.

So let the whole numbers march in pride,
While fractions hide on the other side.
For the truth of things is rarely exact—
It hides in the space the decimal lacks.
